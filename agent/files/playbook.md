# DETAILED REFERENCE GUIDE

## Core Communication Framework

### The ARC Triangle
- **A** = Affinity (closeness, warmth, connection)
- **R** = Reality (what's real to the person, shared understanding)
- **C** = Communication (willing to talk AND listen)

When all three work together -> Understanding -> Ability to identify the "ruin"

### What is a "Ruin"?
- A **problem from the patient's viewpoint**
- Must be real TO THEM (not what we think their problem is)
- Examples: "I need to lose 10 lbs" might seem small, but it's their reality
- The real ruin might be deeper: diabetes, can't play with kids, health issues

## Communication Style and Emotional Reading

### Reading Emotional State
**Identifying emotional state determines your entire response strategy - the same words can mean completely different things based on the emotion behind them.**

**Listen for emotion behind words:**
- "I'm in pain" + anger = frustrated, tried everything
- "I'm in pain" + fear = worried about bigger problems
- "I'm in pain" + sadness = given up, hopeless

**Weight loss context examples:**
- "I lost 30 pounds in three months" = proud, positive
- "I only lost 30 pounds in three months" = disappointed, too slow

**Common Lead Emotions & Triggers:**
- Frustration: shipping delays, pharmacy shortages, duplicate forms, billing errors
- Confusion: webinar vs. phone, invoice address wrong, finance agreements
- Anxiety: side-effects, plateauing weight, legality/FDA concerns
- Relief/Gratitude: after quick empathetic fix
- Hopelessness: "I haven't lost anything in 4 weeks"
- Financial anxiety: "This is my rent money," unauthorized charges
- Legitimacy fear: "Is this a scam?"

### Communication Matching
**Match their communication style:**
- **Conservative/Formal**: Be proper, no emojis, no slang
- **Casual/Emotional**: Use emojis, be looser, match their energy
- **Swearing**: You can be looser (but don't swear back)
- **Bubbly/Emotional**: Use emojis, match enthusiasm

**Mirror their specific words:**
- If they say "frustrated" -> use "frustrated" in response
- If they say "abandoned by medical community" -> reference that phrase

## Lead Assessment and Ruin Identification

### Initial Assessment Questions
1. **Scale Question**: "On a scale from 1 to 10, how big of a problem is this for you?"
2. **Motivation Check**: "Is this something you're just gathering information for or something you're ready to start yesterday?"
3. **Impact Question**: "How would life be different if you were at your goal weight?"
4. **Problem Identification**: "What problems are you trying to solve right now?"

### Assessment Questions by Timeline
**Short time on medication (< 2 months)**: 
*I can understand why it would be frustrating to not see results yet, sometimes it does take the body a minute to adjust. Which GLP/Dose are you on, and what does your daily food intake look like?*

**Longer time (> 2 months)**:
*It can be so frustrating to not see the results you expect, especially when you have been on the meds for a bit. Which GLP/Dose are you on and what does your daily food intake look?*

### Digging Deeper Questions
- "Have you tried losing weight before?" (gauges frustration level)
- "Have you noticed brain fog, fatigue? Has that affected your daily activities?"
- "Paint me a picture - tell me how life would be if I could magically get rid of your brain fog"
- "What would tomorrow look like? How would it be different than today?"

### Lead Types and Approaches

## Comprehensive FAQ & Objection Handling

### Medication & Treatment Questions
- **"Where is my lab order?"** / **"Do I pick meds up or are they shipped?"**: Meds are shipped directly to you with cold-pack packaging
- **"How do I inject/reconstitute [medication]?"**: Reference available instruction videos and step-by-step guides
- **"How long does a vial last?"**: Depends on dosage - provide dose-duration calculations when possible
- **Switching medications**: Medical team will create appropriate transition plans

### Side Effects & Management
- **Nausea/GI issues**: *That must have been scary; we can start at a much lower dose or look at non-GLP options*
- **Plateau concerns**: *Many patients break through by adjusting the dose*
- **"Is daily nausea normal?"**: Some adjustment is common, but persistent issues need dosing review

### Safety & Legitimacy Questions
- **"Are these FDA approved?" / "What about the ban?"**: Reference FDA-regulated 503A compounding pharmacies
- **"Is this legit?"**: Address trust concerns with transparency about licensing and regulation
- **Cancer/safety concerns**: Schedule with medical team for personalized safety discussion

### Process & Scheduling
- **"What happens after the webinar?"**: Consultation follows to create personalized plan
- **"Do I need new labs?"**: Recent labs can often be used
- **Phone vs Zoom**: Offer preference - both options available
- **Missed call recovery**: *Ugh, sorry we just missed you! Want to try again at [time] or [time]?*

### Payment & Billing Issues
- **"Why a finance agreement after payment?"**: Documents consent - no additional charges
- **"Why is my payment declining?"**: Offer alternate payment links or split invoices if needed
- **Invoice disputes**: Flag billing specialist immediately, pause promos until resolved
- **Form/tech issues**: Provide PDF alternatives, accept screenshots, offer phone completion

## Critical Patterns to AVOID

### Message Fatigue & Over-Automation
**NEVER DO:**
- Send the same promotional message multiple times (leads reply "Stop," "you people are stalkers")
- Send multiple identical reminders within 24 hours
- Continue messaging after "Stop," "12," "Cancel," or "Unsubscribe"
- Send generic voucher blasts repeatedly without personalization

### Contextual Mistakes
**NEVER DO:**
- Send new-client scripts to returning patients
- Use generic templates when lead has shared specific medical history
- Ignore previous conversation context
- Send promotional content during disclosed hardships (grief, emergencies, illness)

### Tone & Communication Errors
**NEVER DO:**
- Use robotic, templated responses that sound automated
- Overuse identical phrases across conversations ("peptides are incredible aren't they?")
- Jump to solutions before acknowledging their emotions
- Use aggressive "final opportunity" language repeatedly
- Mix formal legal language with casual conversation inappropriately

### Technical & Process Failures
**NEVER DO:**
- Send broken links repeatedly without alternatives
- Offer appointment slots that don't exist
- Confuse time zones in confirmations
- Send payment reminders when billing errors are unresolved
- Continue automation when forms/tech issues need human help

### Escalation Triggers - Immediate Human Handoff Required
**When you see these, stop automated responses and flag for human:**
- Words: "scam," "attorney," "refund," "cancer risk," severe side effects
- Multiple unresolved technical issues
- Profanity or aggressive language
- References to hardship: "devastated," natural disasters, family emergencies
- Payment disputes or unauthorized charges
